Som Tum (Green Papaya Salad): A spicy and tangy Thai salad made from shredded green papaya, tomatoes, green beans, peanuts, dried shrimp, garlic, chilies, lime juice, and fish sauce.
Pad Thai: A popular Thai stir-fried noodle dish made with rice noodles, tofu, shrimp or chicken, bean sprouts, peanuts, scrambled egg, and flavored with tamarind sauce, fish sauce, garlic, and chili.
Tom Yum Goong: A classic Thai hot and sour soup flavored with lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, lime juice, fish sauce, and chili peppers, with shrimp (goong) being a key ingredient.
Massaman Curry: A Thai curry dish that is rich and flavorful, made with coconut milk, roasted peanuts or cashews, potatoes, bay leaves, cinnamon, star anise, palm sugar, fish sauce, and either beef, chicken, or tofu.
Korean BBQ (Gogi Gui): Refers to the Korean method of grilling meat, typically beef, pork, or chicken, at the table, often accompanied by various side dishes (banchan) such as kimchi, lettuce leaves, and dipping sauces.
Bulgogi: A Korean dish of marinated slices of beef (or pork) grilled or stir-fried, usually served with rice, lettuce leaves for wrapping, and accompanied by gochujang (Korean chili paste) and other condiments.
Bibimbap: A Korean mixed rice dish topped with assorted vegetables (like spinach, mushrooms, carrots, and bean sprouts), sliced meat (often beef), a fried egg, and gochujang (chili pepper paste), mixed together before eating.
Kimchi: A staple in Korean cuisine, kimchi is a fermented vegetable dish (usually cabbage or radish) seasoned with chili peppers, garlic, ginger, and salt, with variations including cucumber or scallions.
Galbi (Kalbi): Korean marinated beef short ribs (or pork ribs) grilled or barbecued, often with a sweet soy sauce marinade, garlic, sesame oil, and sometimes fruit juice, served with rice and side dishes.
Japchae: A Korean dish of stir-fried glass noodles made from sweet potato starch, mixed with vegetables (such as spinach, carrots, and mushrooms) and sometimes beef or other proteins, seasoned with soy sauce and sesame oil.
Pho: A Vietnamese noodle soup consisting of broth, rice noodles, herbs (like basil and cilantro), and meat (usually beef or chicken), often served with lime, bean sprouts, and chili peppers on the side.
Banh Mi: A Vietnamese sandwich on a French baguette, typically filled with various meats (like grilled pork, Vietnamese sausage, or pâté), pickled vegetables (carrots, daikon), cilantro, and mayonnaise or chili sauce.
Bun Cha: A Vietnamese dish of grilled fatty pork (cha) served over white rice noodles (bun) with herbs, pickled vegetables, and a dipping sauce (nuoc cham).
Com Tam: A Vietnamese dish of broken rice served with grilled pork (often ribs or shredded), egg meatloaf (cha trung), pickled vegetables, and fish sauce.
Goi Cuon (Vietnamese Fresh Spring Rolls): Transparent spring rolls filled with shrimp, pork, herbs, rice vermicelli, and sometimes lettuce, wrapped in rice paper and served with peanut or hoisin dipping sauce.
Bo Kho: A Vietnamese beef stew made with tender chunks of beef simmered in a fragrant broth flavored with lemongrass, star anise, and fish sauce, often served with bread or rice noodles.
Cha Ca: A Vietnamese dish of grilled fish (usually firm white fish like catfish or monkfish) marinated in turmeric and dill, served with rice noodles, peanuts, herbs, and nuoc cham sauce.
Laksa: A spicy noodle soup popular in Southeast Asian cuisine, especially in Malaysia and Singapore, made with either curry (curry laksa) or sour tamarind (asam laksa) broth, noodles, fish or shrimp, and garnished with herbs, chili, and sometimes coconut milk.
Nasi Goreng: An Indonesian fried rice dish cooked with kecap manis (sweet soy sauce), garlic, shallots, tamarind, chili, and accompanied by ingredients like egg, prawns, chicken, and vegetables.
Rendang: An Indonesian spicy meat dish originating from the Minangkabau ethnic group of Indonesia, made with beef (or occasionally chicken) slow-cooked in coconut milk and a mixture of lemongrass, galangal, garlic, turmeric, ginger, and chili.
